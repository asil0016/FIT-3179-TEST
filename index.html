🐨Total Energy Consumption in Australia 🐨

This visualisation page shows the total energy consumption in Australia from 2010 until 2020. There are five information or visualisations that relate to the topic. The first visualisation will talk about the total energy consumption based on the type of industry. This will shows how Australia people consumed energy before COVID-19 breakout in Australia.

Total Energy Consumption per State

Below is the map visualisation which shows the total energy consumption in Peta Joule (PJ). In this case, there are 5 states which are New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland, Western Australia, South Australia, Tasmania, and Northern Territory. ForAustralia Capital Territory state,the data was not available from the source The colour saturation shows the value of energy consumption in each state, darker colour shows that the state consume more energy consumption. These data were collected from 2010 until 2020.

Total Energy Consumption by Type of Industry

The bar chart is showing the total energy consumption by type of Industry and per year. The type of industry consists of Transport, Manufacturing, Residential, Mining, Commercial and Services, Agriculture, Other, Construction, Water and waste.The red color bar chart show thatTransport industryis the one who has highest total energy consumption in Peta Joule. Other than that, the bar chart has sorted from the highest to the lowes value of total energy consumption.

Total Energy Consumption by Type of Fuel

The pie chart shows the distribution of energy consumption based on 5 different types of fuel. There are Coal, Electricity, Gas, Oil, Renewables and the value is in percentage value (%). From the pie chart, Oil is constantly the highest consumable fuel and the lowest held by Coal.

Correlation between Total Energy Consumption and Number of Population per Year in each State

The next visualisation is an area chart to shows the total energy consumption per 100,000 population. In this case, Western Australia has the highest value in every year and the lowest held by South Australia.

Correlation between Total Energy Consumption and GSP per Year in each State

For the last visualisation, there is a line chart that showing is there any impact given by GSP (Gross State Product) to energy consumption in each state and over time. From the line graph, it shows there is no correlation between GSP and the energy consumption in every states. Furthermore, there are no impacts given by GSP to the energy consumption.
